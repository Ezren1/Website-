Hadith Contradicts The Qur'an

When secondary sources (Hadith) are placed next to the Qur'an, numerous direct contradictions emerge. This demonstrates their human origin and fallibility, as God's words cannot be inconsistent.

Adultery Punishment

Qur'an (24:2) commands 100 lashes. Hadith command stoning to death.

Apostasy Penalty

Qur'an (2:256) states "No compulsion in religion." Hadith command death for apostates.

Inheritance of Prophets

Qur'an (27:16, 19:6) shows prophets inherit. Hadith claims prophets leave no inheritance.

Hadith Contradicts Hadith

The Hadith collections are filled with internal contradictions, where one "authentic" narration says one thing and another "authentic" narration says the complete opposite. This is a clear sign of human error and unreliability.

Ablution after Cooked Food

One hadith says eating cooked food breaks wudu. Another says the Prophet ate and prayed without new wudu.

The Day Creation Began

One "authentic" hadith says creation started on Saturday. Top scholars reject it as false.

Fasting While Traveling

Some hadith forbid fasting on a journey. Others show the Prophet and companions doing it.

Hadith Attack Prophet's Character

The Qur'an describes the Prophet Muhammad as having the "most exalted character" (68:4). However, many hadith, accepted as authentic, portray him in a manner that is inconsistent with this divine praise, depicting him as forgetful, violent, or even bewitched.

Prophet Under Magic

Qur'an (17:47) refutes the slander that the Prophet was bewitched. A hadith in Bukhari claims he was.

Prophet Forgetting Qur'an

Qur'an (87:6) promises the Prophet he will not forget. Hadith claim he forgot verses during prayer.

A Suicidal Prophet?

A hadith in Bukhari claims the Prophet repeatedly tried to commit suicide. This is an unimaginable attack on his character.

Problematic Hadiths

Beyond direct contradictions, many hadith contain narratives that are scientifically absurd, morally questionable, or promote superstitious beliefs that have no place in the rational and pure religion of God.

Fly in the Drink

A hadith claims one wing of a fly has a disease and the other has its cure. This is scientifically baseless.

Women Nullifying Prayer

Hadith claim a woman, donkey, or black dog passing in front of a praying person invalidates their prayer.

Drinking Camel Urine

A hadith in Bukhari prescribes drinking camel urine for medicinal purposes and describes brutal torture.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common objections and honest answers from a Qur'an-centric perspective, providing clarity and dispelling misconceptions.

How do we pray without hadith?
The Qur'an commands prayer (Salat), mentions its timings, and its core components like standing, bowing, and prostrating (e.g., 2:238, 11:114, 22:77). The universal physical practice of Salat has been passed down through generations from the time of Abraham, a practice Prophet Muhammad revived. The Qur'an confirms these rites. The specific, rigid rituals detailed in Hadith are not required. True prayer is about sincere remembrance of God (20:14), not performing a precise, human-codified ritual.
Doesn't the Qur'an say to "Obey the Messenger"?
Yes, and the Messenger's primary duty was to deliver the message he received from Godâ€”the Qur'an. Obeying the Messenger *is* obeying the Qur'an. He had no authority to create his own separate religious laws. As he was commanded to say: 'I only follow what is revealed to me.' (Qur'an 6:50). Therefore, true obedience to the Messenger is to adhere strictly to the revelation he delivered: The Qur'an, and nothing else.
Isn't this a new sect?
On the contrary, this is a call to abandon all sects (Sunni, Shia, etc.), which are themselves based on allegiance to different sets of hadith and scholars. It is a return to the original, pure Islam guided by the one Book sent to all of mankind, the Qur'an. The Qur'an itself condemns sectarianism: 'As for those who divide their religion and break up into sects, you have no part in them in the least.' (Qur'an 6:159).
Are you saying all hadiths are false?
A hadith may contain historical truth, but it cannot be used as a source of religious law or doctrine. Only the Qur'an is divinely guaranteed to be true and preserved (15:9). Since the Hadith collections contain proven contradictions with the Qur'an, with science, with history, and with each other, they are demonstrably unreliable as a whole. We cannot risk our salvation on a source that is not 100% protected by God.
